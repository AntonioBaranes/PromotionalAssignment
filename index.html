Persona 5 is one of the most revered JRPG's (Japanese Role Playing Game) of all time.
Even though there's way more than just 5, here's 5 reasons YOU should play it!

#2 The Characters

The characters from Persona 5 are widely beloved by the community for their depth, complexity, and relatability.
Each character and their personalities are thoroughly developed through the games plot and confidant system, a unique system that rewards players for spending time with characters outside of the main story.
Each character's confidant ark explores their inner struggles and sees them grow thus giving the player a deeper connection to them.
The strong story and confidant system allow players to develop connections to these characters.
Personally, Yusuke is my favorite.

#3 The Time Managment System

Persona 5 implores a very unique day to day system that allows players to perform a certain amount of actions before the day ends.
This makes it so that the player has to think about what they're going to do that day.
This presents a layer of players-player unique experiences that not many games achieve.
Different players will have completely different experiences playing the game depending on how they decide to manage their time which I think is really cool.
The system also demonstrating the passing days really makes the player feel that time is passing which helps immersion in a way only a video game could.

#5 The 1 More/Baton Pass System

Persona 5 is a turn based rpg which means that the player can take their time chosing what action they will take.
The same system is used in the extremely popular pokemon games.
This includes the type effectiveness system used in them too.
Think of how if you hit a grass type with a fire move it would be super effective in pokemon.
The same is true for persona but the system goes even further.
When an enemy is hit by a move they are weak to the attacking party member is granted a One More.
As the name implies it gives them one more action before their turn ends.
However, this extra turn can be given to a different party member while also buffing their attack in what's called a baton pass.
Then this party member can attack a different enemy with a weakness and keep passing the baton.
The more the baton is passed the higher the attack buff.
If all enemies are downed the party can unleash a devastating AOE (area of effect/attacks all enemies on field) called an all out attack.
By using the one more, baton pass, and all out attack, one can end an encounter before even getting hit which conserves the parties hp.
This system, which as far as I know is unique to Persona 5, heavily favors strategy and finding an opponents weakness and is also so fun!
